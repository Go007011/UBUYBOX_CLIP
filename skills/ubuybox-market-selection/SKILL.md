# UBUYBOX Market Selection

## Overview

Use this skill to choose the right market before list pulling begins. The goal is not to pick the cheapest market. The goal is to pick a market where cash buyers, landlords, flippers, or institutional activity already make it easier to move deals.

## Decision Standard

Approve a market only when both of these are true:

- the metro has enough buyer activity to support fast dispositions
- the team can define a practical hunting zone inside that metro

Reject or delay the market when buyer demand looks thin, vague, or purely hypothetical.

## Workflow

1. Start with the target metro, not the property list.
2. Confirm there is an active buyer pool.
3. Prefer markets with evidence of cash buyers, landlords, flippers, or institutional activity.
4. Narrow the search to target zones about 30 to 45 minutes outside the major city when that improves deal flow.
5. Return a short ranking of the best candidate markets and why each one qualifies.

## What Good Looks Like

- Buyers are already transacting there.
- The area supports the current buy box.
- The radius is specific enough for list pulling and calling.
- The recommendation explains why deals should move fast there.

## Output Format

Return:

- target metro
- buyer-pool rationale
- preferred submarket zone
- why this market should move faster than the alternatives
- risks or missing data
- go/no-go recommendation

## Guardrails

- Do not recommend a market just because it is cheap.
- Do not move into list building until the buyer side looks strong enough to support dispositions.
- If buyer demand is unclear, say that the market is not yet validated.
- If the target area is too broad to source efficiently, narrow it before approving the market.
